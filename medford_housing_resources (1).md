## EMERGENCY SHELTER OPTIONS (If You Get Kicked Out)

### Understanding Shelter Types

**EMERGENCY SHELTER:**
- **Purpose:** Get you off the street IMMEDIATELY
- **Duration:** Overnight to a few weeks (short-term)
- **Access:** Walk-in, usually 24/7, no appointment needed
- **Services:** Bed, food, basic safety
- **Examples:** Medford Navigation Center (Kelly Shelter), OHRA
- **Reality:** Congregate setting (shared rooms), temporary solution

**TRANSITIONAL HOUSING:**
- **Purpose:** Bridge to permanent housing with support services
- **Duration:** 6 months to 2 years (longer-term)
- **Access:** Application process, intake required
- **Services:** Case management, job help, life skills training, housing search assistance
- **Examples:** Salvation Army Hope House, Rogue Retreat programs
- **Reality:** More structured, requires participation in programs, goal is permanent housing

**Which do you need?**
- **If kicked out TODAY:** Emergency shelter (Navigation Center)
- **If you have time to plan:** Apply for transitional housing (Hope House)
- **Best strategy:** Use emergency shelter while waiting for transitional housing placement

## RV / VAN LIVING OPTION

### The Reality of Vehicle Living in Medford

**LEGAL RESTRICTIONS:**
Medford Municipal Code 10.859 states: **You CANNOT legally live in an RV, trailer, or vehicle anywhere in Medford except in a designated RV park.**
- Can't park on streets and live in it
- Can't park on private property outside RV parks (unless it's your own land with proper permits)
- Police can cite you and impound vehicle

**This means vehicle living is NOT a free option - you still need to pay for RV park space.**

### Buying a Vehicle to Live In

**PROS:**
- Your own space, privacy
- Can lock the door
- Can set your own schedule
- Better than shelter for some people
- Can keep belongings secure

**CONS:**
- Upfront cost to buy vehicle
- $600-800/month for RV park space (same as cheap room rental)
- Vehicle maintenance, repairs, insurance
- Limited space, no real bathroom/shower unless in RV park
- Winter = cold, summer = hot
- Still need RV park (can't just park anywhere)

**COSTS:**

*Used RV/Travel Trailer (Craigslist Medford):*
- 1978-1997 older models: $3,850-$10,000
- 2002-2003 models: $10,000-$22,500
- Condition varies widely - may need repairs

*Cargo Van Conversion (DIY):*
- Used cargo van: $3,000-$8,000
- Conversion costs: $500-$2,000 (insulation, bed platform, storage)
- Stealth camping potential (looks like work van)
- Still illegal to park on streets overnight

*Minivan (Cheapest):*
- Used minivan: $2,000-$5,000
- Remove seats, add mattress
- Very basic, no amenities
- Better than nothing

**Reality check: $3,000-$5,000 minimum to get into vehicle living, PLUS $600-800/month for legal parking.**
